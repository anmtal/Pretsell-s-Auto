Plain English

What your light could mean

The same amber light covers dozens of causes — from a $0 fix to a bigger repair. Here are the common ones. We confirm which is actually yours before quoting a thing.

Loose or faulty gas cap

The most common trigger of all — and often the cheapest. A loose, cracked or worn cap breaks the fuel-system seal and lights the dash.

Oxygen (O2) sensor

Reads exhaust to balance the fuel mix. A tired sensor hurts fuel economy and can fail emissions — usually a straightforward replacement.

Catalytic converter

Cleans your exhaust. It rarely fails on its own — often it's a symptom of another issue we'll trace back to the real source.

Spark plugs / misfire

Rough idle, hesitation or a stumble under load often points to worn plugs, coils or wires. Caught early, it's an easy, affordable fix.

Mass airflow (MAF) sensor

Measures the air coming in so fueling stays right. Dirty or faulty MAF sensors cause rough running and poor mileage — often just a clean or swap.

EGR / emissions system

Recirculates exhaust to cut emissions. Clogged valves or leaks trip the light; we test the system and tell you exactly what it needs.
